Who was YouTuber Annabelle Ham?

SINCE the mid-2010s, Annabelle Ham has been posting content on YouTube for thousands of fans to enjoy.

Tragically, the young star passed away in July 2023 at the age of 22, causing many to wonder what happened.

Who was YouTuber Annabelle Ham?

Annabelle Ham was a popular social media influencer and YouTuber based in Atlanta, Georgia.

She first got her start on social media after she created a YouTube page in May 2014.

Since then, she has gained over 77,000 subscribers and her videos have received over 1.9million views.

Ham also had a similar following on other social media sites like Instagram, where she would often post pictures about her life at Kennesaw State University and her travels.

What happened to her?

It was announced on July 17, 2023, that Ham had suddenly passed away.

Her death was first confirmed in a Facebook post by the Alpha Omicron Pi fraternity at Kennesaw State University, which revealed that she had died two days prior.

"Alpha Omicron Pi is mourning the loss of Annabelle Ham, Beta Zeta (Kennesaw State U), who passed away on July 15, 2023," the post read.

"Annabelle lived a vibrant life, brought smiles into every situation, and was cherished by all who knew her.

"Our sisterhood is completely heartbroken for the Ham family and for a beautiful life tragically cut short."

Despite confirming the young star's death, the fraternity did not provide a cause.

Some online reports claim that she died in a car accident, but that information has not been confirmed as of this writing.

What content did she have on her YouTube?

Ham had a bubbly personality which was often displayed in her YouTube videos.

Her page was filled with vlogs of her daily life as well as fashion and makeup advice.

In Ham's last video, which was posted three weeks before her death, she discussed her journey with eyebrow microblading, which is a form of permanent makeup.

"Today is going to be a fun [video] because I'm so excited to finally sit down and finally chat with you guys about something super cool," she said in the almost 18-minute clip.

"For a long time now, I've wanted to get my eyebrows done... It was always just something I felt insecure about and I'm definitely the type of person that feels if you have the opportunity, the ability, the money, to go out and have work done that will make you feel more confident about yourself, I'm all for it."

She went on to explain the procedure so her fans knew exactly what they could expect if they wanted to get something similar done.

Her other videos included vlogs of her trips to places like Colorado, California, and Florida, as well as videos of her trying on clothes for each season.
