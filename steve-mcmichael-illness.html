Steve McMichael Illness: What Happened to Him?

Stephen McMichael, also known as “Mongo” or “Ming the Merciless,” is a former American football player, coach, politician, sports broadcaster, and wrestler. He played college football for the Texas Longhorns and had a successful 15-year career in the NFL, winning Super Bowl XX with the Chicago Bears.

McMichael also wrestled professionally from 1995 to 1999 and made a comeback in 2008, notably as part of WCW’s Four Horsemen. After retiring from wrestling, he coached the Chicago Slaughter from 2007 to 2013 in the Continental Indoor Football League.

In 2013, McMichael ran for mayor of Romeoville, Illinois, but unfortunately, he didn’t win the election. There are concerns among his fans about his health, and they want to know whether he is doing well or not. This post provides all the information related to his health.

Steve McMichael Illness Illness

Steve McMichael has been diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), also known as Lou Gehrig’s disease. ALS is a progressive neurodegenerative disease that affects nerve cells in the brain and the spinal cord, leading to the loss of muscle control.

Over time, this can result in severe physical disabilities, including paralysis. McMichael publicly revealed his diagnosis in 2021, bringing attention to his battle with this challenging illness.

What Did Steve McMichael Say About His Illness?

Steve McMichael has been open and reflective about his battle with ALS since his diagnosis in January 2021. He said,

“I’m not going to be out in the public anymore … you’re not going to see me out doing appearances, hell I can’t even sign my name anymore, and everybody’s going to be speculating, ‘Where’s McMichael, what’s wrong with him?’ I’m here to tell everyone I’ve been diagnosed with ALS, Lou Gehrig’s Disease, so I’m not going to be a public figure anymore.”

Is Steve McMichael Currently in the Hospital?

No, Steve McMichael is not currently in the hospital. He has returned to his home in Homer Glen after being released from the hospital, where he was being treated in the ICU for sepsis and pneumonia.

His wife frequently posts photos of him on her Instagram account. In a recent Instagram post, she shared a picture with the caption,

“Steve and Jim Osborn having a great visit.”

What is ALS?

Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS), often referred to as Lou Gehrig’s disease, is a progressive neurodegenerative disease that affects nerve cells in the brain and the spinal cord.

The motor neurons degenerate and die, leading to a loss of voluntary muscle action. This can cause patients to lose the ability to speak, eat, move, and even breathe. The exact cause of ALS is unknown, and it primarily affects adults, typically between the ages of 40 and 70.

There’s no cure for ALS, and treatments focus on managing symptoms and improving quality of life. The progression of the disease varies, with some patients living much longer than others, but it is typically fatal.
