What Is Bad Bunny Religion? Ethnicity And Origin

Benito Antonio Martínez Ocasio, AKA Bad Bunny, hails from the tough streets of Almirante Sur in Vega Baja, Puerto Rico, where he was born on March 10, 1994.

His Father, Tito Martínez, a Dominican, worked as a truck driver, while his mother, Lysaurie Ocasio, was a dedicated educator before retiring.

Talking about Bad Bunny Religion, he is a Catholic Christian, and his religion is one of the primary reasons behind his interest in the music industry.

During his childhood, he regularly attended church services, accompanying his deeply religious mother. He was actively involved in the church choir, showcasing his vocal talents until age 13.

Following his departure from the choir, the Puerto Rican Reggaeton Singer fostered a keen fascination for the musicians he listened to on the airwaves, particularly emphasizing Daddy Yankee and Héctor Lavoe.

Bad Bunny Is Very Active in Humanitarian Acts

In 2019, Bad Bunny received a nomination for the “Humanitarian Award of the Year” in the inaugural edition of Premios Tu Música Urbano, organized by Telemundo.

Bad Bunny joined over half a million Puerto Ricans, including Residente and Ricky Martin, in a protest against government corruption on July 22, 2019. They shut down the Luis A. Ferré Highway and demanded the resignation of Governor Ricardo Rosselló.

In July 2018, Bad Bunny criticized a Spanish nail salon for refusing him service due to his gender, sparking homophobic comments from some of his fans.

Bad Bunny criticized Don Omar’s homophobic tweet in Jan 2019. He raised awareness about the murder of transgender woman Alexa Negrón Luciano on The Tonight Show in Feb 2020 with a shirt reading “They Killed Alexa. Not a Man in a Skirt.” 

In the aftermath of Hurricane Maria’s destructive impact on Puerto Rico, Bad Bunny was vocal in expressing his dissatisfaction with the insufficient humanitarian assistance.

He set up the Good Bunny Foundation, an organization to provide underprivileged children in Puerto Rico with toys and other essential resources.
