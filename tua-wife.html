Tua Wife: Everything You Need to Know About the Dolphins QBs Spouse

Tua Tagovailoa, an electrifying young quarterback in the NFL, has captured the hearts of fans with his remarkable arm talent and leadership abilities ever since he made his debut in the league in 2020.

Beyond his on-field exploits, Tua also leads an intriguing personal life.

Let’s delve into the details of his wife, Annah Gore, the woman who has captured Tua’s heart.

Who Is The Wife Of  Tagovailoa Tua?

Annah Gore, born on June 11, 1999, in High Point, North Carolina, is the spouse of Tua Tagovailoa.

Their clandestine wedding took place in Florida on July 17, 2022, a mere 10 days before the commencement of the Dolphins’ training camp.

Unfortunately, their marital union was inadvertently unveiled by a radio host on August 2, 2022, which left Tua disheartened as he had hoped to maintain the privacy of his personal life, considering it to be an intrusion.

Annah’s parents are Christina, a realtor, and Gary Gore, a private wealth CEO.

She shares her family with four siblings: Ally, Grace, Garrison, and Ayden. Annah’s journey with Tua began during their high school days, and both pursued their education at the University of Alabama.

In 2021, she earned a degree in business administration.

Beyond her academic accomplishments, Annah is an entrepreneur who manages her own online boutique known as “Annah G Boutique.”

Her entrepreneurial spirit is a testament to her ambition and creativity.

Annah Gore is not only Tua’s life partner but also a dedicated supporter of his athletic career.

She frequently shares images of him on her Instagram account and attends his games, passionately cheering for him from the spectator stands.

In her words, Tua is not just her husband but also her best friend, and her affection for him is boundless.

Does Tua Have A Baby?

Tua and Annah celebrated the birth of their first child on June 11, 2023, a meaningful date as it coincided with Annah’s birthday.

Their newborn son was given the name Tuanigamanuolepola Tagovailoa Jr., a homage to Tua’s own full name, though they affectionately refer to him as TJ for short.

Tua proudly shared the news of their son’s arrival on his Instagram account, posting a heartwarming picture of himself cradling the baby in his arms.

His heartfelt caption read, “Wishing a happy birthday to my beautiful wife and introducing our handsome son to the world.

Words can’t capture the overwhelming sense of blessing you both bring into my life.

“My love for you both knows no bounds.”

Tua spoke about how becoming a father had transformed his perspective on life, deepening his appreciation for children.

He expressed that witnessing his son’s radiant smile and joyous laughter had become his greatest source of happiness.

Tua was eager to set a positive example and be a strong role model for his beloved son.

Where Is Tua Now?

With the fifth overall pick in the 2020 NFL Draft, the Miami Dolphins selected Tua Tagovailoa.

His NFL debut came in Week 6 of the 2020 season when the Dolphins faced off against the New York Jets.

It was in Week 8 against the Los Angeles Rams that he threw his first touchdown pass.

Tua’s rookie season showed promise, as he guided the Dolphins to a 10-6 record, narrowly missing out on a playoff spot.

During that year, he completed 186 out of 290 passes for a total of 1,814 yards, throwing 11 touchdowns while encountering five interceptions.

He also showcased his mobility by rushing for 109 yards and scoring three touchdowns on the ground.

As he heads into his second season, there are high expectations for Tua.

He has been diligently working during the offseason to recover from his college hip injury and to strengthen his chemistry with his receiving corps.

Notably, his coaches and teammates have commended his strong work ethic, leadership qualities, and overall maturity.

The Dolphins are counting on him to lead the team to a playoff berth in the upcoming season.

How Do You Pronounce Tua’s Last Name?

Tua’s surname, Tagovailoa, originates from Samoan heritage.

He received this name in honor of his paternal uncle, who was named after a devoted sports enthusiast known for his substantial weight of 500 pounds.

Interestingly, his last name, Tagovailoa, carries the meaning “the solitary mountain peak.”

To accurately pronounce Tua’s last name, say it as “tung-o-vai-LOA.” The initial part resembles the word “tongue,” while the final part rhymes with “aloha.” Stress should be placed on the last syllable.
